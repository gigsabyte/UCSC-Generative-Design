Assignment 5: Music with Markov Chains

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Markov generation of classical music based on the works of Johann Sebastian Bach, Johannes Brahms, Domenico Sarlatti, and Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky.

When a composer is added to the sample set, a MIDI collection of their music is translated to an array of plaintext strings. This array of strings is then used to train the Markov generator based on the frequency of notes (or rests) appearing in sequence.

For example, every the generator observes a quarter note of A5, it adds the next note after it to a list of valid notes to appear after A5. After all notes have been processed, the frequency of each note that comes after A5 is then normalized to a value between 0 and 1. If the Markov chain generates the note A5, it then chooses the next note to generate based on these frequencies.

Markov chains work best with large volumes of data that is relatively specific. Because of this, you might notice that adding more composers actually makes the music sound worse.

Not that classical was ever good to begin with...
